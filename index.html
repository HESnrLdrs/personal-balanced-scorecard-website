What is a Balanced Scorecard?

The Balanced Scorecard was developed by Kaplan and Norton in 1992 as a strategic management tool for organizations. Instead of focusing solely on financial metrics, it measures performance across four balanced perspectives:

  • Financial - How do we look to shareholders?
  • Customer - How do customers see us?
  • Internal Process - What must we excel at?
  • Learning & Growth - Can we continue to improve and create value?

This framework has helped thousands of organizations achieve strategic clarity and balanced performance measurement.

Why Adapt It for Personal Life?

Professor Sarah Thomson's insight: "We use sophisticated strategic planning at work, but wing it with our personal lives. What if we applied the same systematic thinking to life balance?"

üìö How to Use Your Strategic Life Balance Scorecard

üìä Taking the Assessment

  • Be Honest: Move sliders (1-10) based on your current reality, not where you want to be
  • Think Recent: Rate based on the last 2-3 months, not just this week
  • Consider Context: Rate relative to what's realistic for your life stage and circumstances
  • No Perfect Scores: Very few people score 10/10 - that's normal and healthy

üéØ Understanding Your Results

  • 8.0+: Excellent strategic balance - you're thriving across all areas!
  • 7.0-7.9: Strong balance - minor tweaks will optimize your life
  • 6.0-6.9: Good foundation - some areas need strategic attention
  • 5.0-5.9: Moderate balance - time for strategic life planning
  • Below 5.0: Needs attention - perfect time to apply your planning skills to your life

üìã Setting Effective Goals

  • Start Small: Pick 2-3 actions per area maximum - you can always add more later
  • Be Specific: "Exercise more" becomes "Walk 20 minutes after dinner 3x/week"
  • Use Professor Thomson's Guidance: Click the expandable sections for expert insights
  • Follow the 90-Day Plan: Implement gradually for sustainable change

üí° Getting the Most from This Tool

  • Monthly Reviews: Retake the assessment monthly to track progress
  • Save Everything: Download PDF reports to track your journey
  • Focus on Balance: Don't aim for perfect scores - aim for sustainable balance
  • Strategic Mindset: Apply the same planning rigor you use at work to your personal life
